# Chest X-Ray Interpretation Guidelines (Demo)

## Common Findings

### Consolidation
- Opacification of lung parenchyma
- Air bronchograms may be present
- Compare with prior for interval change

### Pleural Effusion
- Blunting of costophrenic angle
- Meniscus sign on lateral view
- Compare quantity with prior study

### Cardiomegaly
- Cardiothoracic ratio > 0.5 on PA view
- Note: AP views appear magnified
- Compare with prior for interval change

### Pneumothorax
- Visible pleural line
- Absence of lung markings peripherally
- URGENT: Report immediately if large or tension

## Comparison Protocol
1. Always reference prior study when available
2. Document interval changes explicitly
3. Use standard terminology: improved, stable, worsened
4. Quantify changes when possible
